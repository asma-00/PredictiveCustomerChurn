Binary Features and Their Relationship to Churn summary¶

The stacked bar plot and churn rate analysis provide a clear view of how various binary features relate to customer churn.

Key insights include:

  • Senior Citizen
    • Churn rate is significantly higher for senior citizens (41.7%) compared to non-seniors (23.7%).
    • p-value < 0.0001 indicates this is statistically significant.
    • Suggests age-related factors may influence retention strategies.
  • Multiple Lines
    • Customers with multiple phone lines show a slightly higher churn rate (28.6%) than those with a single line (25.1%).
    • While the effect is smaller, p-value = 0.0009 confirms it’s statistically meaningful.
  • Online Security, Backup, and Device Protection
    • Customers without these services churn at much higher rates:
      • Online Security: 31.4% (No) vs. 14.6% (Yes)
      • Online Backup: 29.2% (No) vs. 21.6% (Yes)
      • Device Protection: 28.7% (No) vs. 22.5% (Yes)
    • All p-values < 0.0001.
    • These findings highlight the retention potential of bundled security features.
  • Tech Support
    • A striking difference: churn is 31.2% without tech support vs. only 15.2% with it.
    • Reinforces the importance of accessible customer support.
  • Streaming Services (TV & Movies)
    • Customers using streaming services tend to churn more:
      • TV: 30.1% (Yes) vs. 24.4% (No)
      • Movies: 29.9% (Yes) vs. 24.4% (No)
    • May indicate that entertainment services alone do not build loyalty.
  • Partner and Dependents
    • Strong negative relationship with churn:
      • Partner: 33.0% (No) vs. 19.7% (Yes)
      • Dependents: 31.3% (No) vs. 15.5% (Yes)
    • Could suggest family commitments lead to more consistent service usage.
  • Phone Service
    • Minimal difference in churn rate (26.7% vs. 25.0%), and p-value = 0.3499 indicates no significant impact.
  • Paperless Billing
    • Customers using paperless billing churn far more (33.6%) than those who don’t (16.4%).
    • This may reflect broader behavioral trends, Emails get ignored (spam folder), Lack of physical reminders (accidental non-payment), or demographic trends—possibly more tech-savvy but less loyal users.

Summary

The analysis reveals that service features such as tech support, online security, and having a partner/dependents are protective against churn, while streaming services and paperless billing are positively associated with higher churn. These insights can inform targeted retention strategies, such as incentivizing tech support or bundling security features for high-risk segments.

Recommended Actions:

  • Promote support services: Encourage adoption of tech support, online security, and device protection — these features are strongly linked to lower churn.
  • Target senior citizens: Develop retention strategies tailored to older customers, who churn at significantly higher rates.
  • Engage streaming users: Monitor and improve satisfaction among customers using streaming services, who show slightly higher churn.
  • Review paperless billing strategy: Investigate why paperless billing users churn more — consider clearer communication or loyalty incentives.
  • Leverage family-based stability: Prioritize marketing to or retaining customers with partners or dependents, as they are less likely to churn.
  • Low-Priority: Phone service (phoneservice) has negligible impact → No intervention needed.

  • Internet Service Type (internetservice_No)

  • Payment Methods (paymentmethod_Mailed check, paymentmethod_Credit card (automatic))

  • Dependents
    • Customers with dependents show lower churn, suggesting they may value stability and bundled services more.

  • Internet Type - Fiber Optic (internetservice_Fiber optic)
    • Surprisingly, fiber optic users are more likely to churn — possibly due to price sensitivity or service issues.
  • Monthly Charges and Total Charges
    • Customers paying higher monthly charges tend to churn more, while total charges are less influential, perhaps due to their correlation with tenure.

  • Promote long-term contracts to boost retention.
  • Investigate churn reasons among fiber optic users.
  • Encourage engagement with support and additional services.
  • Facilitate easy and automated payment methods.
  • Target high-monthly-charge users with loyalty programs or tailored offers.
